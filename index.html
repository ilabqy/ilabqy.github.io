Interactive Stereo Segmentation

Stereo DataSet

The purpose of this dataset is to test segmentation algorithms on stereo image pairs. The dataset consists of 37 pairs of stereo images and their additional data, including ground truth images, input strokes and disparity maps (as shown in the figure below).

Image No.1 to image No.30 are from the Adobe Stereo Selection Dataset [1], which can be downloaded from http://www.adobe.com/go/datasets. Note that there is one pair omitted compared to the original 31 pairs in [1], since we cannot find the pair’s images in the above website. Image No.31 and No.32 are selected from [3,4]. The remaining five pairs (No.33-37) are selected from the dataset in [2]. Images of No.31 to No.36 could be downloaded from the following position:

No.31:2006 dataset [3,4] (http://vision.middlebury.edu/stereo/data/scenes2006/):baby3
No.32:2005 dataset [3,4] (http://vision.middlebury.edu/stereo/data/scenes2005/):Reindeer
No.33:dataset [2](http://web.cecs.pdx.edu/~fliu/project/stereo-saliency/):#4
No.34:dataset [2](http://web.cecs.pdx.edu/~fliu/project/stereo-saliency/):#403
No.35:dataset [2](http://web.cecs.pdx.edu/~fliu/project/stereo-saliency/):#460
No.36:dataset [2](http://web.cecs.pdx.edu/~fliu/project/stereo-saliency/):#605

All the additional data can be downloaded here. This dataset has been used in [6].

We present a new method in the paper “Interactive Stereo Image Segmentation with RGB-D hybrid Constraints” submitted to IEEE Signal Processing Letters. Compared to the other methods, our method utilizes two more clues, disparity distribution (DispDis), and disparity variance (DispVar). Here are the AERs (average error rates) of segmentation with only DispDis, with only DispVar, and with both of the clues (our method), and the AERs of three state-of-the-art methods [1][5][6] on each stereo pair.
